Some journeys are planned for the destination, while others become memorable because of the people, the unexpected moments, and the stories they leave behind. Our four-day trip to my friend Rakibul's home near Santahar was exactly that kind of adventure.

## Day 1: The Journey Begins

On the morning of **6 October 2024**, I, Jasem, Saad, Mahedul, and Rakibul woke up before sunrise to catch our train. I have always loved traveling by train. There is something peaceful about watching villages, rivers, and endless green fields pass by while the rhythmic sound of the wheels accompanies your thoughts.

During the journey, I immersed myself in *Nokkhotrere Rat* by **Humayun Ahmed**. Between chapters, I walked to the train restaurant and enjoyed a warm cup of tea while watching Bangladesh glide past the window. We spent the rest of the ride laughing, sharing stories, and making jokes that only close friends could appreciate.

When we finally reached Rakibul's home, just a five-minute walk from Santahar Railway Station, we discovered something that surprised all of us. Throughout our university life, we had never realized that Rakibul came from such a well-established family. It was one of those funny revelations that made us wonder how little we sometimes know about our closest friends.

After resting for a while, we explored the town. It was remarkably small—you could walk across the market in less than twenty minutes. Yet its quiet charm made it special. Sitting beside the historic Santahar Junction, the town carried an atmosphere where railway history and everyday life blended naturally.

That night was filled with laughter, ghost stories, and conversations that stretched long after midnight.

## Day 2: A Ghibli-Like Morning

The next morning gave us one of the most magical experiences of the entire trip.

We visited the abandoned train graveyard, a vast area stretching for nearly two kilometers. Rusting locomotives, forgotten railcars, and overgrown tracks stood quietly among lush greenery. Nature had slowly reclaimed the place, making it feel as though we had stepped into a Studio Ghibli animation.

We climbed old cranes, explored abandoned trains, and wandered through the silent landscape with endless curiosity. Every corner looked like a photograph waiting to be taken.

In the afternoon, we hired a local van and set off to explore the surrounding countryside. The scenery was breathtakingly rural, with endless stretches of golden-green paddy fields swaying gently in the breeze. Along the narrow village roads stood traditional mud houses, simple yet beautiful, reflecting a way of life that has endured for generations. The atmosphere was incredibly peaceful, far removed from the noise of the city, and we passed several rice mills that reminded us how deeply agriculture shapes the region's economy and daily life.

As evening approached, we returned to town. Rakibul took us to a local tea stall famous for its rich milk tea, and we enjoyed cup after cup while chatting about the day's adventures. Later, we walked onto the quiet, dark railway line and sang songs under the open sky. Surrounded by silence, broken only by our voices and the occasional distant sound of a train, it became one of the simplest yet most unforgettable moments of the journey.

## Day 3: Walking Through History

The following day, we visited *Mahasthangarh*, one of the most significant archaeological sites in Bangladesh.

Walking among the ancient ruins, we imagined the civilizations that had flourished there centuries ago. The old walls, historical remains, and open landscapes created a fascinating connection between the past and the present. It was more than sightseeing—it was like stepping into a living history book.

That night, Jasem and Saad said goodbye and headed home, while Mahedul and I stayed behind because our train was scheduled for the next day.

## Day 4: Roktodan Beel and an Unexpected Adventure

Before leaving Bogura, Rakibul insisted on showing us one final destination: **Roktodan Beel**.

The wetland was breathtaking. We crossed to the other side by boat, surrounded by still water, soft breezes, and complete tranquility. It was one of those rare places where time seemed to slow down completely. Away from the noise of cities, we simply enjoyed the silence and the beauty of nature.

Eventually, Rakibul escorted us to the railway station, and we said our goodbyes, believing our adventure had come to an end.

But the journey had one final surprise waiting for us.

Our train was supposed to take us back to Khulna. Instead, when we woke up the next morning, we discovered that we were almost in Dhaka! Rakibul had accidentally put us on the wrong train.

Mahedul's reaction was priceless—he was absolutely furious, while I could hardly stop laughing at the absurdity of the situation.

Getting home turned into another adventure. We got off at Dhaka Airport Railway Station and struggled to find bus tickets. Since it was only two days before Eid, transportation was packed and tickets were incredibly difficult to obtain. After plenty of waiting, searching, and a bit of luck, we finally managed to secure seats and make our way home.

## Looking Back

When I think about this trip now, I don't just remember the destinations. I remember reading a favorite book on a moving train, sipping tea while watching the countryside, wandering through a train graveyard that looked like a dream, singing beside silent railway tracks at night, crossing a peaceful beel by boat, exploring ancient ruins, and laughing over the unforgettable mistake that sent us toward Dhaka instead of Khulna.

It was four days filled with exploration, friendship, unexpected twists, and memories that will stay with me for a lifetime.

Sometimes, the best journeys aren't the ones that go exactly according to plan—they're the ones that leave you with stories worth telling.
