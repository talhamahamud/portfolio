This Is Why I
Started Writing!

December 24, 2025 Talha Mahamud 5 min read

Writing is one of those things I kept putting off. There was always a reason — not enough time, not enough knowledge, not enough confidence that what I had to say was worth anyone's attention.

But then I started noticing something: the moments I understood something deeply were almost always the moments I tried to explain it to someone else. Teaching forces clarity. And writing, I realized, is just a more permanent form of that.

The Real Reason

I study physics. Every day I encounter ideas that are genuinely astonishing — and genuinely hard to hold onto. Writing forces me to slow down. To translate the mathematical into the verbal. To find the metaphor that makes the abstract feel real.

If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it well enough.

I'm not sure Einstein actually said that. But it doesn't matter — it's true. And it describes exactly what I want writing to do for me.

What I'll Write About

Mostly physics and mathematics. Sometimes machine learning. Occasionally philosophy, because the boundaries between these fields are more porous than the textbooks suggest.

I won't always get things right. Some posts will probably be embarrassingly naive in retrospect. But that's fine — the log of confusion is just as valuable as the log of clarity.

An Invitation

If you're reading this: thank you. You didn't have to be here. I'll try to make it worth your time.
