This Is Why I Started Writing!

Published on 2025-12-24

Why I Started Writing?

I did not start writing because I wanted to be a writer.
I started writing because I wanted to understand.

From physics to mathematics, from machine learning to questions about human nature and the universe — my mind is constantly occupied with why and how. Writing became the space where all these questions could breathe, evolve, and sometimes even find clarity.

Curiosity That Never Rests

I deeply love physics, mathematics, and machine learning. These subjects are beautiful, but they are also demanding. Many times, I find myself stuck — staring at an equation, a concept, or a line of code that refuses to make sense.

Instead of moving on, I pause.

I try to break the idea down, strip it of unnecessary complexity, and rebuild it from the ground up. Writing helps me do exactly that. When I can explain something in simple words, I know I truly understand it.

From Confusion to Clarity

Most of what I write starts from confusion.

Whenever I struggle to understand a concept, I imagine someone else standing at the same point — equally confused, equally curious. My goal is not to sound smart or advanced, but to present ideas in the simplest and most honest way possible.

If something confused me, it will likely confuse others too. Writing becomes my way of saying:

“You’re not alone. Let’s figure this out together.”

Loving Simple Explanations

I believe clarity is a form of respect.

Complex ideas do not need complex language. Whether it’s a physics concept, a mathematical intuition, or a machine learning model, I enjoy explaining things in a way that feels natural and accessible.

I write the way I wish I had learned — slowly, patiently, and without unnecessary jargon.

Thinking About Human Nature and the Universe

Beyond equations and algorithms, I am deeply fascinated by human nature and the universe itself.

Why do we think the way we do?
What does it mean to understand reality?
How does the human mind try to make sense of an infinite universe?

Writing allows me to explore these questions freely. It’s a bridge between science and philosophy, between logic and curiosity.

Writing as Self-Development

At its core, writing is a tool for self-development.

Every blog post sharpens my thinking, improves my communication, and forces me to be honest about what I know — and what I don’t. Writing teaches me discipline, clarity, and humility.

I grow with every sentence I write.

Why I Continue Writing

  • I write to learn.
  • I write to explain.
  • I write to think.
  • I write to grow.

This blog is not a showcase of expertise — it is a reflection of my journey. A journey through physics, mathematics, machine learning, human nature, and the endless curiosity that drives me forward.

And as long as I keep asking questions, I will keep writing.
